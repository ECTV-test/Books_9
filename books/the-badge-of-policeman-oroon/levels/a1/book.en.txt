[[CHAPTER:]]
Sometimes, people love at first sight.
This love can be risky.
They might not know each other well.
But it happens, and this story is about that.

During a war, the Gentle Riders became famous.
They were wild men from the West and rich men from the East.
In uniform, they looked the same and became friends.

Ellsworth Remsen was from New York and had a lot of money.
He joined the Gentle Riders.
The war was fun for him, so he did not miss his games and meals.

One trooper was a calm young man named O’Roon.
Remsen liked him a lot.
They rode together in a famous charge.

After the war, Remsen went back to his life.
One day, O’Roon came to his club.
They greeted each other like old friends.

“Can you get me a job, Remsen?” O’Roon asked. “I have no money.”

“No problem,” said Remsen. “What job do you want?”

“I walked in Central Park today,” said O’Roon. “I want to be a mounted policeman. I can ride. Can you get me that job?”

Remsen said he could help.
Soon, O’Roon was a mounted policeman in the park.
Now, let's talk about love at first sight.

Remsen was walking on Fifth Avenue.

A car moved slowly because of traffic.
Inside was a chauffeur, an old man, and a young lady.
The lady was very beautiful.
Remsen saw her and knew he was in love.
The car moved on, and Remsen hoped to see her again.

Remsen went to a Gentle Riders reunion.
There were speeches, food, and drinks.
O’Roon was there too.

“I’m drunk, Remsen,” said O’Roon. “I can't walk straight. I have to work soon. It's over for me.”

“Look at me,” said Remsen, smiling. “Who do you see?”

“Good old Remsen,” said O’Roon.

“No,” said Remsen. “You see Mounted Policeman O’Roon. I will wear your badge and do your job today.”

Remsen, pretending to be Policeman O’Roon, rode into the park.
He enjoyed his day.

In the morning, a carriage came down the drive.
Inside was the old man and the lady Remsen loved.

Remsen met them as they passed.
Her eyes met his, and she blushed.
Then the horses ran away.

Remsen chased them.
He stopped the horses.
The driver held the horses.
The old man talked, and the lady's eyes thanked Remsen.
They asked his name and thanked him.

Remsen felt proud.
He opened his mouth to speak but stopped.
Who was he? Mounted Policeman O’Roon.
If he told the truth, O’Roon would be in trouble.
Love came, but friendship came first.

Remsen touched his cap and spoke.

“Don’t mention it,” he said. “We do our duty.”

He rode away, knowing he did the right thing.
At the end of the day, Remsen returned the horse.
He went to O’Roon’s room.
O’Roon was smoking a cigar.

“I wish you and the police were at the devil,” said Remsen.

O’Roon smiled.

“Good old Remsen,” he said. “I know all about it. They found me here two hours ago. I had trouble at home. I didn’t tell you my father is the Earl of Ardsley. Funny you met them in the park. If you hurt my horse, I’ll never forgive you. I’m going to buy him. Oh, and my sister—Lady Angela—wants you to come to the hotel with me tonight. You didn’t lose my badge, did you? I need to return it when I resign.”
