[[CHAPTER: Chapter 1]]

TWO THANKSGIVING DAY GENTLEMEN

There is one special day. On this day, all Americans go home to eat. President Roosevelt gives us this day. We hear about the Puritans, but we don't remember them well. Plymouth Rocks? That sounds familiar. Many have switched to chickens since turkeys are expensive.

The big city celebrates Thanksgiving Day. The last Thursday in November is a big American holiday.

Now, here is a story. It shows our traditions grow fast.

Stuffy Pete sits on a bench in Union Square. Every Thanksgiving Day for nine years, he sits there at 1 o'clock. Each time, something happens to him.

But today, Stuffy Pete is not hungry. He just ate a big meal. His face is red and full. His clothes are tight. Buttons pop off his shirt. He is full from a big dinner with turkey, potatoes, and pie. He sits and looks at the world.

The meal was a surprise. He passed a big house. Two old ladies live there. They give a big meal to the first hungry person they see. Stuffy Pete was that person today.

After a while, Stuffy looks left. He sees the Old Gentleman coming.

Every Thanksgiving Day for nine years, the Old Gentleman finds Stuffy Pete. He takes him to a restaurant for dinner. The Old Gentleman wants to make this a tradition.

The Old Gentleman is thin, tall, and sixty. He wears black clothes and glasses. His hair is white. He uses a cane.

Stuffy Pete wants to run, but he can't. He is too full.

"Good morning," says the Old Gentleman. "I am glad to see you. Come with me for dinner."

The Old Gentleman says this every year. Stuffy Pete looks at him sadly. But he knows he must go.

The Old Gentleman has no family. He lives alone. He has small hobbies. He feeds Stuffy every Thanksgiving.

Stuffy Pete looks at him. The Old Gentleman is happy to give. Stuffy says, "Thank you, sir. I will go with you."

Stuffy knows he is part of a tradition. He must eat the dinner.

The Old Gentleman takes Stuffy to the restaurant. They sit at the table. The waiters know them.

"Here comes the old man," says a waiter, "who feeds the same man every Thanksgiving."

The Old Gentleman is happy. The waiters bring food. Stuffy eats a lot, even though he is full. He does not want to make the Old Gentleman sad.

After an hour, Stuffy is done. "Thank you for the meal," he says. He stands up and leaves. The Old Gentleman pays the bill.

They say goodbye at the door. The Old Gentleman goes south. Stuffy goes north.

Around the corner, Stuffy stops. Then he falls to the ground.

An ambulance comes. They take Stuffy to the hospital. He ate too much.

Later, another ambulance brings the Old Gentleman. He is very hungry. He has not eaten for three days.
